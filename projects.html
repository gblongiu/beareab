The Longtooth Collective

Forged in the Bay Area underground by guitarist Jon Howell, drummer Dan Sneddon, and bassist Matt O’Brien, The Longtooth Collective was never a band but a spontaneous rupture of noise rock, math rock, and post-hardcore.

They enlisted three vocalists: Paul Isham (Curse of the Zero), Harvey Rañola (Fair Haired Child), and Gabriel Long (beareab). Each vocalist shifting the emotional register with every track. The result is a blistering document of tension and dissonance.

Recorded in 2008 and shelved for over a decade, The Imitation Zone isn’t a comeback but a transmission of jagged time signatures, broken narratives, and the slow collapse of identity under grinding sludge riffs.

Howell’s angular phrasing carves out space, O’Brien’s low end bulldozes through the mix, and Sneddon’s drumming is always searching and shifting. Turning each song into an unrepeatable battleground of intention.

Though short-lived, these sessions echo in bands like Kowloon Walled City, Tigon, Early Graves, Less Art, Ex Everything, and beareab.

Release date: April 4, 2008

  • Guitar: Jon Howell
  • Drums: Dan Sneddon
  • Bass: Matt O’Brien
  • Vocals: Paul Isham, Harvey Rañola, Gabriel Long
